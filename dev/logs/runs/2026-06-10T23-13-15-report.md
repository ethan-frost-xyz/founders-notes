**What to make of the pattern:** Rockefeller's language around competition consistently frames it as something to be *disciplined*, not embraced as a virtue in itself. He doesn't rail against competitors as enemies — he describes them as victims of their own ignorance ("never knew all the facts about his own affairs") or participants in systemic waste ("needless competition," "unnecessary duplication"). His framing is almost clinical: competition is a problem of industrial structure, not personal animus. That's a sharp contrast with, say, a Larry Ellison, who in ep-0124 explicitly says Oracle exists "to beat the competition" — for Rockefeller, competition was a market failure to be solved, not a fight to be won.
