> Who founded Nike?

Nike was founded by **Phil Knight** and **Bill Bowerman**.

Phil Knight was the driving entrepreneurial force. He started the company as Blue Ribbon Sports in 1964, selling shoes out of the trunk of his car — something his father dismissively called "jackassing around" [ep-0010]. Knight's animating philosophy was that work itself should feel like play: "If my life was to be all work and no play, I wanted my work to be play" [ep-0010].

Bill Bowerman was Knight's former track coach at the University of Oregon and co-founder, serving as the obsessive product mind behind the shoes. His engineering philosophy was rooted in relentless marginal gains — he was famously obsessed with reducing shoe weight. As he put it, "One ounce sliced off a pair of shoes is the equivalent to 55 pounds over one mile" [ep-0010]. Bowerman was a first-principles thinker who rejected conventional wisdom in both coaching and design, and he's the one who famously created the original waffle sole by pouring rubber into his wife's waffle iron [ep-0153].

The partnership is classic: Knight brought the business vision and relentless salesmanship, Bowerman brought the coach's understanding of what athletes actually needed and the tinkerer's drive to build it. Knight sought a calling; Bowerman paid for his with nerve damage from years of inhaling rubber cement in an unventilated workshop, leaving him unable to run in the shoes he helped create [ep-0153].
