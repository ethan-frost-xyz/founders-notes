> Who founded Nike?

Nike was co-founded by **Phil Knight** and **Bill Bowerman**.

Knight is the central figure in the Nike origin story — covered extensively in episode 10 on *Shoe Dog*, his memoir. He started the venture in 1964 (originally as Blue Ribbon Sports) after a "crazy idea" epiphany during a morning run in 1962, selling Japanese running shoes out of the trunk of his car [ep-0010].

Bowerman was Knight's track coach at the University of Oregon and his eventual business partner. He was the obsessive engineer of the duo — maniacally focused on reducing shoe weight because he believed "lightness directly translated to less burden, which meant more energy, which meant more speed and speed equaled winning" [ep-0010]. His first-principles thinking about athletics (he pioneered the "hard-easy" training method when it was scorned by other coaches) and his hands-on shoe tinkering — famously creating the waffle sole in his own kitchen — became foundational to Nike's culture and competitive edge [ep-0153].

So it's a classic two-founder dynamic: Knight brought the entrepreneurial drive and vision, Bowerman brought the coach's engineering mindset and relentless focus on marginal gains.
