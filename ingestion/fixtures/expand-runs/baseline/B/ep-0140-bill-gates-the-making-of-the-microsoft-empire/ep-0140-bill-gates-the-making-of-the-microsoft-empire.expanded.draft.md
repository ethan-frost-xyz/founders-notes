### 43:00 — Losing a $50,000 deal is a $100,000 loss

Context: Bill Gates analyzes competitive losses in terms of opportunity cost. He calculates that losing a contract not only forfeits revenue but also strengthens a competitor, effectively making the loss twice as damaging.

Quote: "Most companies like, oh, we lost out on $50,000 in revenue. Bill Gates is like, **no, we lost out on $100,000**. We lost out on $50,000 and the person that we have to knock out of the game just gained $50,000. So it's $100,000 loss. It's not a $50,000 loss to him." (43:00)

Key takeaway: This double-counting mentality forces a founder to treat every competitive loss as a strategic setback, not just a financial one.
