### 1:05:00 — Origin of Chrysler building

Context: Senra explains why Chrysler decided to build the iconic Chrysler Building in New York. The motivation was not vanity but a desire to give his sons responsibility and a meaningful challenge. Chrysler told the architect to make it taller than the Eiffel Tower, and he later gave his son Walter advice on how to learn the building business from the ground up.

Quote: "I came to the conclusion that what my boys ought to have was something to be responsible for. They had grown up in New York and probably would want to live there. They wanted to work. And so the idea of putting up a building was born. Something that I had seen in Paris recurred to me. I said to the architect, **make this building higher than Eiffel Tower.** That was the beginning of the 77 story Chrysler Building." (1:05:00)

Key takeaway: Great entrepreneurs often create legacy projects not for personal glory but to instill responsibility and purpose in the next generation. Chrysler's instruction to exceed the Eiffel Tower's height reflects his lifelong pattern of setting audacious goals, while his advice to his son to start in the basement scrubbing floors demonstrates that true leadership comes from understanding every level of an organization.
