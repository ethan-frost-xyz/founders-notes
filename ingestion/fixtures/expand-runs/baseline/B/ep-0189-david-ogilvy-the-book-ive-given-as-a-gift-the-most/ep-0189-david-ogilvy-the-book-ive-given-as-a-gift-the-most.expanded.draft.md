Context: Still within the AGA Cooker sales manual, Ogilvy advises salesmen on how to handle objections and build trust. This datapoint highlights two specific tactics: leveraging satisfied customers and steering clear of competitor comparisons.

Key takeaway: Social proof—citing happy customers by name—builds credibility without defensiveness, while mentioning competitors introduces negativity and erodes trust. Ogilvy’s rule is to keep the conversation focused on your own product’s merits. The broader lesson is that selling is about aligning with the prospect’s interests, not tearing down alternatives, because mud-slinging reflects poorly on the seller.
