Context: The narrator uses the analogy of Walmart's expansion to illustrate why starting FedEx was so difficult: unlike Walmart, which could open one store at a time, FedEx had to build a nationwide network before taking its first order.

Quote: "He says, like -- I love this analogy, it really for me illustrated how difficult this was, “Like you open a Walmart store, and if that formula succeeds and you do well, you open a 1,000 of them, but **you don't open a 1,000 of them to take the first order**, and that is what FedEx had to do.”" (39:00)
