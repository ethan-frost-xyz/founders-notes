### 34:00 — Business is like nature — it doesn't care how you arrive at the right answer

Context: David Senra reflects on Robert Noyce’s pragmatic, results‑oriented methods at Fairchild Semiconductor, where Noyce favored a “quick and dirty” approach that delivered 90% of the answer in 10% of the time. Senra pulls in a personal notebook quote to reinforce the idea that the marketplace rewards what works, not the elegance of the process.

Quote: “And one of it was a treat, it says, ‘Business is like nature. **It doesn't care if you arrive at the right answer from the wrong reasoning.** Evolution demand survival. It doesn't ask you to show your work.’” (34:00)

Key takeaway: In fast‑moving industries, practical results outweigh theoretical perfection. Noyce’s success came from understanding that getting to a viable product quickly—even if the why isn’t fully understood—is more valuable than waiting for a pristine solution. Founders should bias toward action and iteration, because the market only judges the outcome.

### 1:00:00 — Live accordingly: guard your time as your most precious resource

Context: After quoting Noyce’s advice to “use money to buy time because money is cheaper than time,” Senra connects that philosophy to a Naval Ravikant insight about the finite nature of life. The passage underscores why Noyce, despite his wealth, ultimately advised prioritizing family over material ambition.

Quote: “And he says, ‘Guard your time, it's all you have. Money has a discount rate. As you get older, that discount rate goes up. When you're about to die, the discount rate may be 100%. Most people would give up everything for 1 more week or 1 more year.’ And this last sentence is the most important part, **‘Live accordingly.’**” (1:00:00)

Key takeaway: Time is the ultimate non‑renewable resource, and near the end of life its value becomes absolute. Noyce’s own regrets about his first marriage echo this: no amount of money can buy back lost time with loved ones. The founder lesson is to build a life and business that respect the real value of time, choosing presence and alignment over constant hustle.
