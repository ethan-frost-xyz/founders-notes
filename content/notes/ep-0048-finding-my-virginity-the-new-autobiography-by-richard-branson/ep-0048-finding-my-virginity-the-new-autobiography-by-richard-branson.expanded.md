### 48:00 — Risk and focus: why mistakes often happen in the mundane

Context: After completing the record-breaking GlobalFlyer flight, Steve Fossett died in a routine plane crash. Richard reflects that adventurers are fully focused during extreme risks but can become careless during everyday activities.

Quote: "It's strange how adventurers often die doing something relatively mundane. **When you know the dangers, are trained for them and are fully focused, you are usually ready to deal with them. Mistakes can often happen in less dangerous situations.**" (48:00)

Key takeaway: Complacency in routine tasks is where serious errors occur because the heightened focus required by high-risk endeavors evaporates. This serves as a reminder to maintain rigor and attention even when the work feels easy, preventing costly oversights in business and life.
