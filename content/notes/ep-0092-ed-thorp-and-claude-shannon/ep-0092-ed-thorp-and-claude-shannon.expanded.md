### 28:00 — Kelly formula: even unlikely events eventually occur

Context: As Ed Thorp prepares to test his blackjack system with real money, Claude Shannon recommends the Kelly criterion to size bets. The Kelly approach, developed at Bell Labs, mathematically protects against ruin by insisting that no bet can wipe you out, because seemingly impossible streaks will happen.

Quote: “The core of John Kelly’s philosophy of risk can be stated without math, and it’s as follows. **It is that even unlikely events must come to pass eventually.** Therefore, anyone who accepts small risks of losing everything will lose everything sooner or later.” (28:00)

Key takeaway: The Kelly criterion isn’t just a gambling formula; it’s a survival principle for investors and entrepreneurs. Because fat‑tail events are inevitable, any strategy that tolerates a non‑zero chance of total loss will eventually self‑destruct. The only way to sustain compounding is to cap downside so you never go to zero.
