### 14:00 — Decide quickly, make your pitch, nail down the details

Context: When Durant first encountered the road cart that would launch his carriage empire, he immediately sought to buy the business. The owner offered to sell the whole company, and Durant had to act fast to secure the deal.

Key takeaway: Durant’s approach to deals was to act decisively and not let lack of money stop him from seizing an opportunity. The lesson is to balance bold action with prudent financial planning.

Quote: "But it was almost impossible to put together more than 20 companies of varying profitability and uneven management in 2 years and be entirely certain of what was going on. **Durant had little faith in his central staff, so he didn't have one.** Again, Henry Singleton didn't have a large central staff, Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger don't. Key takeaway: Durant’s refusal to build a competent central staff left him blind to the financial realities of his conglomerate, leading to his downfall.
