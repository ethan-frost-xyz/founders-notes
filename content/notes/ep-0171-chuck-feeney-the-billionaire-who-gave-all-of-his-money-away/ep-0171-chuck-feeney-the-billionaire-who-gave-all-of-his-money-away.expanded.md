### 26:00 — Humble beginnings of the Honolulu duty-free shop

Context: After the collapse of their earlier mail-order liquor and car sales businesses, Chuck Feeney and Bob Miller are starting over with only the small duty-free concession they had won in Hawaii. The raw note “Not much” refers to the physical state of the shop when Feeney arrived to run it in late 1965.

Quote: “The duty-free shops at Honolulu International Airport when Chuck arrived to run it in late 1965 was more -- **a little more than a market stall.** Its floor area was just over 100 square feet.” (26:00)

Key takeaway: Even the most lucrative ventures often begin in unimpressive physical circumstances.
