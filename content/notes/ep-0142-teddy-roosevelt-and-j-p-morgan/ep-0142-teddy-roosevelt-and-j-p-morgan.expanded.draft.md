### 16:00 — Strong Rule the Weak, Wise Rule the Strong

Context: David Senra highlights a lesson from the story of J.P. Morgan and James J. Hill. While Morgan exercised enormous control over financially fragile railroads, Hill’s exceptional management kept his railroad independent. This dynamic reveals a broader principle about power and autonomy.

Quote: “Morgan drew his power from poorly run businesses. And I think a lesson from history that is playing out on the pages that I hold in my hand is that **the strong rule the weak, but the wise rule the strong**. And I think the Burlington is one example of this.” (16:00)

Key takeaway: Financial weakness invites outside control, while well-managed strength creates freedom. Hill’s profitable and efficiently run railroad made him immune to Morgan’s influence, teaching that true power doesn’t come from size or force alone but from wisdom and operational excellence. Build a resilient enterprise, and you’ll never be forced to cede authority to financiers.
