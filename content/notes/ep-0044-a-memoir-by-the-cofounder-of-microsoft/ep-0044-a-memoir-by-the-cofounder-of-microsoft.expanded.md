### 29:00 — Each failure contains the seeds of your next success

Context: After the failure of their first business, Traf-O-Data, Paul Allen reflects on the lessons learned and how that failure prepared them for Microsoft. The raw note captures his takeaway that failure isn’t final if you extract the right lessons.

Quote: "I wanted to pay homage to an obscure piece of hardware that played a critical role in the microprocessor software revolution. **In my experience, each failure contains the seeds of your next success if you're willing to learn from it.** Bill and I had to concede that our future wasn't in hardware or traffic tapes." (29:00)

Key takeaway: Failure only becomes a dead end if you refuse to examine it. Traf-O-Data’s collapse taught Allen and Gates that their real edge lay in software, not hardware, and that competing against free was nearly impossible—a lesson Gates later applied when confronting Linux. Entrepreneurs who treat failure as a classroom instead of a verdict keep moving toward breakthroughs that initial setbacks helped reveal.
