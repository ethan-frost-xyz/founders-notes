### 37:00 — The Real Estate Play That Funded McDonald’s Growth

Context: After realizing that the 1.9% franchise fee on 15-cent hamburgers wouldn’t support the training and R&D needed to maintain quality, Harry Sonneborn devised a plan to make McDonald’s a restaurant development company. The innovation was to lease land and use subordinated mortgages to finance restaurants, transforming the business model.

Quote: "Harry's idea, simply put, was that we could **induce a property owner to lease us his land on a subordinated basis.** That is, he would take back a second mortgage so that we could go to a lending institution and arrange a first mortgage on the building, the landlord would subordinate his land to the building." (37:00)

Key takeaway: By separating the real estate from the operations and using a lease structure that required minimal upfront capital, McDonald’s generated recurring revenue from rents and percentage-of-sales payments while scaling nationwide. This wasn’t just a financing trick—it permanently made McDonald’s a real estate company that sold hamburgers, a strategic shift every asset-light business can learn from.
