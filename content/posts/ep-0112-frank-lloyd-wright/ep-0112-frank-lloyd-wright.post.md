Founders #112: The Greatest American Architect

“If there is no spiritual quality in architecture, it would just be plain lumber.” - Frank Lloyd Wright

Cut corners, and you lose the spirit. 

Soul is the difference between a builder and an artist.

Here is why he stands alone.

Frank Lloyd Wright was a creative anomaly.

He peaked in his 80s.

In just his final decade, he produced 350 designs.

That accounted for a third of his entire life's work.

Works from his second peak include: Fallingwater, the Guggenheim Museum, and the Johnson Wax Building (pictured below).

“Ordinary people cannot live without rules to guide their conduct. I, Frank Lloyd Wright, am not ordinary.” (13:30)

This peak came after spending the previous two decades in despair.

He was broke and lacked respect from his own industry.

Wright reflected:

”My obituaries are all of such nature as to make me want to arise and fight... The comeback seems to be a favorite American institution. From now on, you are to find me actively engaged in that performance.” (1:10:00)

Wright's opinion of himself and architecture.
“If I said, I was the greatest architect in the world, I don't think it'd be arrogant.” (10:00)

“Architecture is the triumph of human imagination over materials, methods, and men, to put man into possession of his own earth.” (11:00)
