Founders #31: Peter Thiel

Thiel went to great lengths to seek righteous vengeance for himself, shutting down a news publication under the guise of privacy rights, while also founding Palantir $PLTR. Ironic.

Episode is ~3hrs and worth every second. Here are my favorite ideas:

“There's no such thing as an obstacle, just information. The earlier you spot and anticipate setbacks, the less demoralizing they will be.” (1:10:10)

Never interrupt an enemy making a mistake. (1:36:50)

“I don’t partner with cynics or pessimists” - Senra (2:07:55)

“Reading is faster than listening” (2:11:28). Note some may need to hear including myself: Don’t take podcasts as supplementation for reading.

“A business with a good definitive plan will always be underrated in a world where people see the future as random.” (2:20:55)

Superior sales and distribution by itself can create a monopoly regardless of product differentiation. But the opposite is not true. (2:26:49)
