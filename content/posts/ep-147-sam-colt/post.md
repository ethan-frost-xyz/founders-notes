Founders #147: Samuel Colt

From selling laughing gas on the street to inventing the revolver and leading the American arms industry, Colt packed a lifetime of extreme hustle, failures, and wins into just 47 years.

Here is the same advice Sam shared with his brother:

Choose a goal and pursue it to the absolute peak.
“Select the object of your ambition and reach its zenith of your hope or die in the attempt.” - Sam Colt

At 32, after a decade of setbacks, Colt told his brother to select your ambition and reach its zenith or die trying. (48:00)

Study human nature deeply to unlock success.
“Life is a thing to be enjoyed. Make up your mind determinedly what station in life you will reach and rely upon it with proper exertion, and you will not be thwarted. Your great study should be of man.”

Understanding people and psychology became the real leverage for selling inventions and building an empire. (49:00)

Never let anyone else govern your destiny.
I love this one: “Lose no opportunity to mingle with the mass and view nature in its most primitive state. If you allow other people to govern you, you subscribe yourself inferior to them. Don't, for the sake of your own good name, think again of being a subordinate.”

Scarred by early authority and a failed board-run company, Colt warned his brother that allowing others to control you marks you as inferior. Absolute self-determination drove him to make every decision in his second company. (48:00)

Aim high or die trying. Mediocrity is unacceptable.
Colt ended his letter to his brother with brutal clarity: “You had better blow out your brains at once and manure an honest man's ground with your carcass than to hang your ambition on such a low peg.”

Better to end it all than hang your ambition on a low peg. (50:00)
