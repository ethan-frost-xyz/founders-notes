Founders #8 Robert Noyce 

1) Noyce’s anti-bureaucratic flat culture, combined with a wave of young talent, made $INTL the symbol of disruption and capable of meeting its own seemingly impossible standards for innovation (Moore’s Law).

2) “The names change, the technology changes, but human nature usually stays the same” (27:30), Senra reiterates the maxim first introduced in episode #3 (Edison).

3) An overlooked bubble: 1960s integrated circuits and calculators. A flood of new entrants, collapsing prices, and failing companies. I can see how the promise of a handheld device that could save hours of work and impact daily life made for the perfect entry point for the masses to adopt integrated circuit devices.
