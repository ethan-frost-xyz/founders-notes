Founders #28: Wright Brothers

The Wright Brothers were voracious readers; there was always room for books in the family’s modest budget (7:00). Often they reread books for retention (18:38). The latter point is why I write these posts. 

The dual benefit of having a social contract with myself to commit to this daily process, combined with reflective note-taking, creates a repository of the points I found most valuable. 

People (myself included) often go through the motions, listening to podcasts, so having that added expectation of writing something for a small audience every single day.

Senra’s own take on reading: “The entire reason I do the podcast because I do believe if people read more, their lives would be better… you can spend 5-20 hours having a one-sided conversation with somebody that's thought deeply about the subject so much that they spend years of their life writing the book.” (7:20)
