Founders #122: Alfred Sloan (General Motors)

Henry Ford built the car for the masses.

Alfred Sloan built the company that beat him and dominated the 20th century.

This is how he did it.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

Comfort is a false god.

Sloan was already successful with his own thriving business.

Instead of staying comfortable he sold it and stepped into the total chaos of $GM.

He widened his horizon on purpose.

Nothing great happens inside the safe zone. (22:30)

Focus only on what moves the business forward.

Sloan protected his time with ruthless discipline.

Almost everything he did served the core mission.

High performers do not let side issues steal energy from their main craft. (37:00)

Groups make policy. Individuals enforce it.

Committees are useful for debate and direction.

Execution belongs to one accountable person.

Sloan never confused the two. (39:00)

Find your niche and widen it.

Sloan refused to fight Ford head-on at the cheapest price.

He placed Chevrolet just above the Model T and steadily expanded that gap as customers wanted more.

Smart positioning plus patience cracks even the strongest competitor. (47:00)
