Founders #65: Kirk Kerkorian (MGM Grand)

Despite becoming a billionaire, Kirk never depended on armies of lawyers for agreements. He considered a handshake a binding agreement. In one case, he turned down a higher offer for a company because he had already shaken on a lower one the day before. (7:39)

Kirk built his initial capital by flying dangerous missions for the Royal Air Force over the North Atlantic during WWII, taking a risky route with a high fatality rate because it paid roughly $1,000 per month (24:40).

Kirk consistently reinvested profits from one venture into the next. After pivoting to buying surplus military planes, refurbishing them, and selling them for parts or as complete units for huge returns, he then built a flight school. He sold that and started a charter airline, which he later flipped into real estate in Las Vegas.

Kirk believed there was no point in placing small bets and always to “bet the limit”. He built the largest hotel in Las Vegas three times during his career, reaching a peak net worth of around $20B in 08. He donated almost all his wealth after his death at age 98.
