Founders #146: Milton Hershey

“After all, what good is one's money unless one uses it for the good of the community and humanity in general.”

This philosophy separated him from other industrialists and cemented his utopian legacy through the sustainable community and school he built.

Here are Milton Hershey’s best ideas:

Rock bottom eliminates fear of failure.
After two major business collapses left him broke and in debt, Hershey found liberation in having nothing left to lose, which fueled his bold third attempt without hesitation. (24:00)

Compounding small improvements creates miracles.
Hershey built his caramel business through obsessive daily tweaks to quality and operations, expanding market share year after year without a single breakthrough moment. (31:00)

Perfect essentials for ultimate scale.
By simplifying his chocolate production to focus on just a few core items, Hershey achieved massive efficiency and low costs that outpaced competitors.

Limiting complexity allows total mastery of what matters most. (41:00)
