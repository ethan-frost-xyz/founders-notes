Founders #150: Sam Walton
When naming his new stores, Walton deliberately chose "Walmart" because shorter names reduced costs for signs, maintenance, and lighting.

Cost control should be a core part of every decision, both personally and professionally. (40:00)

Here are some of Sam Walton’s best ideas:

Simple models succeed when they are executed with discipline.
Warren Buffett pointed out that a business's difficulty doesn’t determine its success. Walton demonstrated this by building an empire around a single, clear strategy (buy low, sell cheap) and applying it with unmatched consistency. (6:00)

Count your pennies, and your dollars will take care of themselves.
“We don't make a dime out of the merchandise we sell. We only make our profit out of the paper and string we save.” - Sam Walton (15:00)

Create advantages others are unwilling to pursue.
Frustrated by long drives between stores, Walton learned to fly a plane to scout locations and manage his growing network faster than any competitor. The key wasn’t the plane itself, but finding practical edges that rivals refused to pursue. (27:00)
