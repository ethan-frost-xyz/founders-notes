Origin of membership discounting in retail. 
The specific mechanism to legally offer discount prices was taken from Eugene Ferkauf, the founder of E.J. Korvette in New York from the 1940s. At the time, fair-trade laws allowed manufacturers to legally force retailers to sell products at a minimum price, preventing discounting. 

Ferkauf discovered that he could circumvent these price-fixing laws by requiring shoppers to sign up for a membership. Sol applied this exact strategy to FedMart, selling a $2 lifetime membership that legally classified his store as a private club. This legal distinction allowed him to ignore manufacturer pricing mandates and sell goods at whatever lower price he chose. (22:00)

This would have occurred otherwise, but it’s interesting to think that Eugene inspired Price, who then taught the strategy to Walton $WMT and Sinegal $COST, who then inspired Bezos $AMZN to launch Prime.
