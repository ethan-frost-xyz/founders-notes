Founders #127: Larry Ellison - $ORCL

Larry Ellison had a very direct way of dealing with problems: Find a way or make one.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

Break on through (to the other side).
A programmer said it was impossible to run cables through the wall. Ellison grabbed a hammer and smashed straight through it. That single act summed up his entire business philosophy. 
Find a way or make one. Just do it. (30:00)

Good marketing beats better tech.
“Aver­age technology and good marketing beat good technology and average marketing every day.” He put sales, distribution, and market share first instead of waiting for the perfect product. (36:00)

Engage in an enlightened pursuit of happiness.
Advice to his son, but really to all of us: “He has to engage in an enlightened pursuit of happiness to figure out what makes him happy. That’s all about how you feel about yourself, how you relate to other people, what your work is, what you create and what you make. We are builders. Human beings are builders. He’s going to have to find something he really wants to build.” (58:00)

This was a strong close to the Livin' like Larry series.
