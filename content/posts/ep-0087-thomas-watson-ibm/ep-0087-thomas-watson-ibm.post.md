Founders #87: Thomas Watson - $IBM

"I don't know if this joke is going to land, but I think Watson was the Kanye West of his day." (43:00)

Prophetic insight by Senra that has aged like fine wine since the episode was uploaded, thanks to one insane twist.

Why? Here are a few things Thomas Watson did:

He wrote about himself in the third person.

He claimed to have a "pronounced gift for public speaking" and stated he was "continually being called upon to exercise it" when he was mainly the one calling upon himself to speak to his employees.

When filling out a survey about himself, he described himself as having “Tremendous vision, indomitable will, unswerving determination”.

He described himself as having a "tall figure with dark, direct eyes that appeared to see through people and situations at first glance."

Twist:
Not mentioned in the episode, Watson was more or less business partners with Hitler until it was considered not cool. He even received the Merit Cross of the German Eagle in 1937 from the German government, which he famously declined in 1940.

The episode isn't very insightful compared to previous ones. So, I figured I would just share this absurdity.
