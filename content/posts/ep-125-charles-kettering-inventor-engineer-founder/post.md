Founders #125: Charles Kettering - $GM

He was a poor farm boy who became one of the greatest inventors of the 20th century and the longtime head of research at General Motors.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

An Ounce of Experimentation Is Worth a Pound of Theory
“The destruction of a theory is of no consequence for theories are only stepping stones. In my opinion, an ounce of experimentation is worth a pound of theory.” 
Build, test, fail, adjust. Kettering hated committees because they slowed down real work. (49:00)

Knowing vs Understanding
There is a massive difference between memorizing facts and truly understanding how things work. Kettering learned real engineering from the practical men running the steam engine at the local flour mill, not from any classroom. (14:00)

Improve It Every Single Time
The actor who played Rip Van Winkle for decades taught him the secret to staying passionate: “I made up my mind that I would try to portray Rip Van Winkle just a little bit better each time.” 
That constant effort at improvement kept his interest alive for life. (20:00)

Gain Altitude to Kill Ego
“Everyone ought to take a ride in an airplane. If the general manager of some great factory reaches an altitude of 5,000 feet and sees his factory the size of a postage stamp, he is bound to realize that he is not so much after all.” 
Perspective is the best cure for self-importance. (46:00)
