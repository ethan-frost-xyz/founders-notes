Founders #109 Adi Dassler - Adidas

True innovation often appears as common sense in hindsight.

Adi noticed Olympic runners wore generic shoes.

Adidas dominated the podium. Even the Soviets chose the capitalist spikes.

Sounds obvious 100 years later, but it wasn’t back then:

Family conflicts formed blind spots that Nike exploited.
The Dassler family was so caught up in their internal feud between Adidas and Puma (Adi's brother's company) that they completely overlooked the threat of a new American competitor gaining market share. (50:00)

While $ADS targeted elite athletes, $NKE saw the huge potential in expanding the market to everyday people, effectively creating the jogging category.

The biggest growth often comes not from better serving existing customers (elite athletes), but from redefining who the customer is (making everyone an athlete). (1:00:00)

Nike forgot this concept when $LULU took over the everyday consumer market by establishing the rise of athleisure wear in the 2010s.
