Founders #138: Alexander Graham Bell

He refused to have a telephone installed in his own study.

The man who invented it understood better than anyone that the device itself was the greatest threat to the concentration required to invent it.

Here are some more ideas from Bell:

Trust your own judgment above all.
"A man's own judgment should be the final appeal in all that relates to himself." (10:00)

Connect ideas from unrelated fields.
Bell's powerful imagination led him to synthesize concepts across domains, culminating in the telephone.

You do not need to be classically trained in a single field if you have the imagination to combine separate concepts into a novel solution. (14:00)

Choose a spouse who complements your weaknesses.
Bell's steady wife grounded his passionate ups and downs, handling what he lacked just as crucial business partners did. (40:00)

Bell was a tremendous father.
"He believed that play is nature's method of educating a child and that a parent's duty is to aid nature in the development of her plan." Foster curiosity and exploration over rigid authority. (50:00)
