Founders #83: Tesla, Edison, & Westinghouse

Had he been transferred to another planet with everything against him, he would have worked out his salvation.” Tesla, describing George Westinghouse, the lesser known of the trio, yet equally impressive.

"My one ambition is to be able to work without regard to the expense. I want none of the rich man's usual toys. I want no horses or yachts. I have no time for them. What I want is the perfect workshop." - Edison (33:20)

Be a wolf, survive anywhere you are dropped. (1:12:00)

Charles Coffin, the head of the Thomson-Houston Company that eventually merged with Edison to form General Electric, sold shoes for a living before running $GE. You can literally do anything you want. (1:21:00)

More on Westinghouse:

He actually beat Edison head to head in the electricity business. Edison made the critical mistake of trying to play the same game as his competitor (Westinghouse). (1:22:00)

“I never think of the past, I go to sleep thinking only of what I'm going to do tomorrow.” (1:38:00)

“The crisis through which we are passing is only part of our day's work.” (1:40:00)

During an era when most wealthy business leaders were despised as robber barons, Westinghouse stood out as a man of integrity. When he died, hundreds of his former employees attended his funeral. Having learned about many of his peers, he is truly one of a kind. (1:41:00)
