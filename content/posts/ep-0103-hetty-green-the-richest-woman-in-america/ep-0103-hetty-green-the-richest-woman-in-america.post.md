Founders #103 Hetty Green - Investor

“The markets may change, the methods may be revamped, but as long as human beings are propelled by greed and ego, they are doomed to repeat the mistakes of the past.”

In 1907, she single-handedly bailed out New York when major banks failed:

Her ideas on managing your money:

“Watch your pennies and the dollars will take care of themselves.” (2:45)

When offered a bargin, always take the evening to reflect before accepting the deal. (3:30)

Most people watch their money wash away in the flood. (18:00)

She was an all-american badass:

Hetty meets with rival Collis Huntington (a partner of Leland Stanford), but the meeting turns hostile when he threatens her son, Ned, causing her to drop her business persona and pull a gun on Huntington. (47:30)

Charlie Munger’s inversion thinking… but from 100 years before:

As Hetty ages and hands over control to her son, she provides him with a list of “don'ts” rather than “dos”. Here is the list:

Don't cheat in business dealings. “For sooner or later, conscious will trouble you and your worries up until into your grave.”
Don't fail to be fair in all things, both in business and otherwise.
Don't kick a man when he is down.
Don't envy your neighbors.
Don't overdress, whether you have the means or not, because this causes envy.
Don't fail to go to church.
Don't forget that riches dishonorably gained must be left behind someday.
Don't forget to be charitable.
Don't falsify.
(58:00)
