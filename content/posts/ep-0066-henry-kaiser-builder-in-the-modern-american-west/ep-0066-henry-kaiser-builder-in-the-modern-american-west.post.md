Founders #66: Henry Kaiser (Industrialist)

Kaiser built the Liberty ships for the Allies during WWII (a gold standard for the current industrialists trying to revive American shipbuilding). He also revolutionized modern healthcare, became a steel giant, and constructed the Hoover Dam. Additionally, he jumped off a moving train to submit a bid for a government contracting job. Listen to this episode if any of those elements interest you, and if not, still give it a listen. Here are my datapoints:

Produce more things for more people (8:52)

He made thousands of sales calls in his life, but none were as tough as the first one (14:03)

Focus full effort on getting hired by one person instead of spreading effort across many (23:37)

If you don’t quit, your experiences will build up (32:18)

Find a way to express your passion; it’s a key skill (36:51)

Grand Cooley Dam job (54:10)

The harder something is, the less competition there is (56:50)
