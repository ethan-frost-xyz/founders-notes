Founders #59 Howard Hughes (aerospace engineer, film producer, business magnate, and world record pilot)

The foundation of the Hughes empire was the work of Howard Hughes Sr., a man mechanical by nature who rebelled against a desk job to find fortune in the “frontier of opportunity”.

Howard Hughes Jr is on his own at 18 years old. Following the sudden death of his father, the teenage Hughes seized total control of the family business.

Despite owning a massively profitable tool company, Hughes’s lack of financial oversight and personal extravagance led to a liquidity crisis.

During World War II, Hughes partnered with industrialist Henry Kaiser (also a fascinating guy) to build the world's largest flying cargo ship to avoid enemy submarines.

The crash of the XF-11 spy plane in Beverly Hills marked the definitive end of Hughes's vibrant life and the start of his opioid addiction. It was his fourth crash.

He reminds me of Wilt Chamberlain, a god-gifted talent, but so self-centered that he never reaches his full potential. Wilt also liked to drive fast.
