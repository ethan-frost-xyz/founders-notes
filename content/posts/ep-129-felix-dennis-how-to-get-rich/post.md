Founders #129: Felix Dennis (How to Get Rich)

If you do not believe in yourself at a fundamental level, no one else ever will.

It’s a prerequisite to success.

If money doesn’t drive you, learn his ideas. And if it does, well…

Here are Felix’s best ideas:

Full commitment unlocks clairvoyance.
"The moment that one commits oneself, providence moves too... Whatever you do or dream you can, begin it." - Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749-1832)

Hesitation kills more ideas than anything else. (25:00)

“Rich by accident” is a myth.
Wealth never arrives by lucky accident or by simply following your passion and hoping for the best. 

It is the result of a deliberate, sustained decision to go after it with everything you have. (13:00)

Act without fear of loss.
"You have almost nothing. And therefore, you have almost nothing to lose."

All the great founders leverage this. (16:00)

Never trust the vast mountain of conventional wisdom.
"Conventional wisdom daunts initiative and offers far too many convenient reasons for inaction, especially for those with a great deal to lose."

The status quo exists mostly to protect people who already have something to lose. 

The rare times it is wrong are exactly where the biggest fortunes are made by those willing to ignore it. (17:00)

Life is not a rehearsal.
Wasting decades in work you hate just because other people told you it was the safe or respectable choice is one of the saddest things a person can do. (33:00)

Only you know your inner demons.
"Do not mistake desire for compulsion. Only you can know the song of your inner demons."

The grind required to get truly rich is brutal and long. That level of drive cannot be copied from someone else. (36:00)

The chase for wealth is absurd.
"If you chase money desperately in the earnest belief that you can never be happy without it and seriously think that the chase is a meaningful occupation, I doubt very much you will succeed… an appreciation of the absurdity of the chase helps enormously." (46:00)

Sell Up and Change Your Life
Time is the only asset you can never replace. 

Staying in a business or career that makes you miserable to keep making money is a form of self-inflicted insanity. 

Get out the moment you realize it. (52:00)

Ownership is half the law.
"If on my deathbed, I had only a very short time to pass, to pass on what wisdom I have accumulated about getting rich to a son or daughter, then it would be this. Ownership shall be half the law, doing an outstanding job should be the other half. There's no point in owning 100% of a rubbish company." (54:00)
