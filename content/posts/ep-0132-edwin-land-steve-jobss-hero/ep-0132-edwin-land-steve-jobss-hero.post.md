Founders #132: Edwin Land - Polaroid

Edwin Land was no ordinary founder.

He was the mentor to Steve Jobs.

And helped design the U-2 spy plane.

Here are Land’s best ideas:

If anything is worth doing, it is worth doing to excess.

Land approached his goals with intense passion, living this quote throughout his life. "There's a rule that they don't teach you at the Harvard Business School. It is, if anything is worth doing, it's worth doing to excess." (8:00)

Believe a market will come to exist for any worthwhile product.

Land prioritized creating the best products over immediate market demand. (15:00)

Do not do anything that someone else can do.

This motto guided Land's life and Polaroid's focus on originality. They even scrapped projects if competitors got there first. (21:00)

Be scientifically daring and financially conservative.

Land ran Polaroid with massive scientific risks but zero outside debt. He was able to maintain full creative control. (46:00)

Strive to know more than anyone else about your particular craft.

Like the 17-year-old Land who read every optics book in the library.

Study the history and know the pioneers. (50:00)

Be comfortable saying I do not know.

Despite his intense control, Land showed intellectual humility. He admitted unknowns without hesitation. (52:00)

The value of concentration.
"I find it very important to work intensively for long hours when I am beginning to see solutions to a problem. At such times, atavistic competencies seem to come welling up..." (54:00)

Everything is connected.

Land viewed business, happiness, and doing good as one holistic pursuit.

Not separate compartments.

He does not see business as this separate thing.

He sees everything is related to everything else. (57:00)
