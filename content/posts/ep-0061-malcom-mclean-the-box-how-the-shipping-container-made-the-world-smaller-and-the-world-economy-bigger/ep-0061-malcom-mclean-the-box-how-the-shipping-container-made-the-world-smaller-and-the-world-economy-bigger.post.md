#61 Malcolm McLean (The Father of Modern Shipping)

McLean pioneered modern container technology and built a business around it that reduced handling costs and time, making global trade much more efficient. Early on, McLean faced fierce resistance despite the clear advantages, as ports hesitated to adopt his system, with dockworkers fearing mass job losses due to “automation.” 

JFK said at that time on the topic: “I regard it as a major domestic challenge really of the 1960s, to maintain full employment at a time when automation is replacing them.” Prophetic. In 2024, there was a massive port strike involving over 47K dockworkers across 36 ports, the largest since 1977. History rhymes.

People don’t want the technology, they want the solution it provides (4:55)

It sometimes takes an outsider to change an industry. Malcom came from the trucking sector. (8:20)

There was a lot of resistance, but the genie was out of the bottle (10:58).

Rational people don't risk what they have and need for what they don't have and don't need. (32:20)

Sometimes it’s better not to overanalyze, or you’ll talk yourself out of it (49:05).

Starting with a roughly $400M net worth in 1982, McLean wiped out his wealth and accumulated about $1.3B in personal debt by 1986 by investing his exit funds into an oil container ship business. It’s only too late if you copy (1:12:42).
