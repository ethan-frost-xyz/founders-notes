Founders #7 Ray Kroc.

1) Great ideas often stem from constraints. Before buying out the McDonald brothers, Kroc had limited flexibility to change the business, as every decision needed written approval from both brothers. Many key innovations came from this limitation, the most significant being purchasing or leasing sites for McDonald’s restaurants and then renting them to franchisees. Kroc gained steady cash flow and full control over them, since breaking McDonald’s standards could mean losing the lease. Today, $MCD ranks as the fifth-largest corporate landlord in the world by asset value.

2) Any time is the right time to become an entrepreneur. Kroc was 52 when he joined McDonald’s.

3) When Ray was training for Vietnam, he was with a man in his camp who would spend his weekends on base drawing pictures instead of going out with the others. That man was Walt Disney. (11:38)

4) How you do one thing reflects how you do all things . (40:30)

5) “Business is not like painting a picture, you cannot put a final brushstroke and then hang it on the wall…” Kroc remained deeply involved in McDonald’s until he passed away. (55:00)
