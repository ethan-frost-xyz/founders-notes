Lessons from Founders #10 Phil Knight, founder of $NKE

1) “History is one long processional of crazy ideas.” (7:36)

2) What Phil calls the secret of happiness, “Play… The secret of happiness, I'd always suspected, the essence of beauty or truth or all we ever need to know of either, lay somewhere in that moment when the ball is in midair, when both boxers sense the approach of the bell… There's a kind of exuberant clarity in that pulsing half-second before winning and losing are decided. I wanted that, whatever that was, to be my life, my daily life.”

3) Bowerman (Nike co-founder) was obsessed with losing weight; it was a simple equation: “‘One ounce sliced off a pair of shoes,' he said, 'is the equivalent to 55 pounds over one mile.’” Bowerman eventually designed the original waffle shoe, not before becoming the coach of the US Olympic track team.

Simplicity in goals (or lightness for Bowerman) clarifies decision-making.

4) You should do what you love says Knight. Senra adds, “I'm shocked at how many people don't even try.” Underrated point.

5) “If you’re following your calling, the fatigue will be easier to bear. The disappointments will be fuel. The highs will be like nothing you've ever felt.” PK (63:00)

8/10
