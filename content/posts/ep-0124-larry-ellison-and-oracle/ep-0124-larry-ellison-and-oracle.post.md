Founders #124: Larry Ellison - $ORCL

"Life is the only miracle. I don't waste a lot of time. I work intensely on things that I care about, and I spend my time with people that I care about. I do what I want because there's not much time left."

Here is how to be Livin' like Larry

David Senra described Larry Ellison as the MJ of B2B SaaS, working competitively and living his life just as hard when out of it.

He needed enemies to stay sharp.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

Choose Independence.
"I love golden retrievers, but I don't want to be one. I'd much rather be a cat." Dogs obey for food. Cats survive on their own. He rejected blind loyalty and chose independence. (7:30)

Pure misfit.
When told he would look back on his school days as the best years of his life he replied:
"If that's true, I'm going to kill myself right now." (10:30)

Solve problems fast. 
Contract programming let him finish work quicker than anyone, wear what he wanted, ride his motorcycle, and spend time on what he actually enjoyed. (13:00)

The executives are not geniuses. 
He expected brilliant leaders at the top of tech. Instead he found fear, bureaucracy, and paralysis. That gave him the confidence to run his own company. (17:00)

The harder the task the less competition.
He deliberately chose to build the first commercial relational database because everyone else thought it was too risky and impossible. He picked the path others called impossible. (23:30)

All that matters is what is right, not who.
On Bill Gates: "All he cared was what was right, not who was right. That's what makes Bill very, very dangerous." (1:02:00)

Protect the core team.
Turnover is healthy in many areas, but never on the small group building the main product. That accumulated knowledge compounds for decades and cannot be replaced. (56:00)

You cannot lead with uncertainty.
"You cannot lead if you're filled with uncertainty." People follow conviction, not hesitation. (1:08:00)
