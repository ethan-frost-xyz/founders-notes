Founders #111: David Geffen

“Anybody who thinks money will make you happy, hasn't got money” - David Geffen

He lied, cheated, and screamed his way to a billion-dollar empire.

The only soul he had was in the records he sold.

He is a case study in how to win the wrong game.
 
He tried to engineer his own mythology.

Geffen desperately wanted a biography to canonize him as a business hero like Warren Buffett.

Yet by abusing the author, he ironically ensured the opposite happened.

The book instead became a permanent testament to his sociopathic ruthlessness.

Geffen's life was marked by immense financial success, matched only by his regret.

From his youth, primary filter for life decisions was monetary reward rather than interest or talent.

This set a precedent for his life, where he constantly prioritized money over fulfillment, leading to long-term dissatisfaction. (24:30)

He loved the film industry, but left it for music. I bet if he stuck it out he would have been equally successful doing what he loved.

He is the first subject in Founders to lie to everyone for an advantage.

He later told his biographer: “I don't have the burden of bullshit lies, and deceit and cheating none of that plagues me because I don't do any of it” (1:02:00)

Oh the irony.
