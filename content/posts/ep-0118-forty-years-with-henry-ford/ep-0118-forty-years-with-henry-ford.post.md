Founders #118: Henry Ford - $F

Henry Ford had one obsession.

A car so cheap that the average man could own one.

He didn’t just build the car. 

He built the entire system to make it possible.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

Be ruthless on the goal. Flexible on everything else.
Ford never budged on “a car for the masses.” But the how? Whatever worked. Trial. Error. Scrap it. Start over. Stubborn on the destination, wide open on the route. (31:30)

The answer you need is probably already in the graveyard of old ideas.
Ford took a transmission design everyone had thrown away years earlier, dusted it off, and used it to slash costs. History is full of these moments. The future belongs to people who study the past. (1:09:00)

Your best work comes when the odds are stacked against you.
Ford was at his absolute peak when everyone told him the Model T was impossible. Once he won and the empire was running, his attention wandered. Humans are wired for the fight, not the victory lap. (29:00)

The mission never ends.
After the Model T, Ford needed a new mountain immediately or he started to drift. Same as JFK after the moon landing. Finish one impossible thing, then lock in the next one before momentum dies. (31:00)

Experts are often the enemy of progress.
Ford fired anyone who started calling themselves an expert. Their brains filled up with “that’s impossible.” He wanted people who didn’t know what couldn’t be done. Beginner’s mind wins. (44:00)

New ideas need protection.
Ford literally walled off a secret room on the third floor, put a lock on it, and told Sorensen (the author) “we’re starting something completely new in here.” The Model T was born in a maternity ward. (1:08:00)
