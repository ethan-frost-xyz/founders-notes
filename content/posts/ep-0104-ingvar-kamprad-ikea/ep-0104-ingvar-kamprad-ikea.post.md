Founders #104 Ingvar Kamprad - IKEA

Think of time wasted in minutes not hours or days.

“Time is your most important resource... 10 minutes once gone are gone for good. Divide your life into 10-minute units and sacrifice as few of them as possible.” (1:17:15).

9 Rules of IKEA:

1. Product range is our identity.
Don’t create a product and hope people buy it, identify your target customer’s constraints (ex. price). Your product range is your primary communication tool, so if it is confusing, your brand identity will be affected too.

2. The IKEA spirit.
Build a living culture based on your natural roots or spirit. IKEA leadership is expected to work on the floor with employees.

3. Profit gives us resources.
Treat profit as the price of your independence. External capital means less control.

4. Reaching good results with small means.
Constraints are the mother of innovation. McDonald’s real estate empire was built through constraints from the original founders. As a result, Ray Kroc turned $MCD into the fifth-largest corporate landlord in the world by asset value. (Founders #7)

5. Simplicity is a virtue.
IKEA fights complexity by keeping the organization flat and the rules short. Complexity kills speed.

6. Doing it a different way.
Question every requirement aka first principles thinking.

7. Concentration.
Focus your resources on specific targets rather than spreading them too thin.

8. Taking responsibility.
“Only those who are asleep make no mistakes... .. Making mistakes is the privilege of the active.” - Kamprad (32:00)

9. A glorious future.
Avoid stagnation by believing that the majority of the work still remains to be done. Kamprad’s approach to tackling the Innovator's Dilemma.

Other datapoints:

Great way to learn what you want to do is by learning what you don’t want to do first. (10:30)

Kamprad deeply regrets missing his kids childhood for work. “Childhood does not allow itself to be reconquered.” (26:15)

“The decisive moment in the growth of a company is when the founder has to hand over the financial function to somebody else.” (50:45)

Full quote on time: “Bear in mind your time, time is your most important resource... 10 minutes is not just 1/6th of your hourly pay, 10 minutes is a piece of yourself. Divide your life into 10-minute units and sacrifice as few of them as possible in meaningless activity.” (1:17:15)
