Founders #153: Bill Bowerman

The legendary Oregon track coach and Nike co-founder transformed running by rethinking training, shoes, and life itself.

He also once pinned a seven-foot rattlesnake in his driveway with just a clipboard.

Here are Bill Bowerman’s best ideas:

First principles thinking outperforms industry norms.
When the athletic world saw rest as a weakness, Bowerman studied performance from the ground up and demonstrated that strategic recovery was crucial.
This revolutionary approach quickly spread worldwide among elite athletes. (17:00)

Total dedication always carries a personal cost.
Years of experimenting with toxic shoe materials in unventilated spaces left Bowerman with permanent nerve damage and unable to run in the shoes he created for the world. (1:10:00)

Study great lives daily to improve your own.
Just as running every day builds the body, reading biographies every day builds the mind with proven lessons. 
Bowerman and Phil Knight both saw daily habits like these as the way to make the world better. (1:16:00)
