Founders #141: Arnold Schwarzenegger

Moving to the US, he had one year in LA to prove himself, developing an righteous mindset.

"My mission in America was clear. I was on a path. I needed to train like hell, diet like hell, eat well and win."

The power of deadlines.

Here are three more you should know:

Reps are everything.
Juggling construction, business, and college in LA, Arnold drilled posing transitions obsessively to avoid any slip in competitions. 

Raw repetition builds skill where talent falls short. When you have talent and raw repetition you get Arnold.

"You're not born good at something... you have to practice, practice, practice. You practice every pose and every transition because that extra step is the very thing that can make you lose in front of the judges." (26:00)

Lucille Ball's advice for Arnold. I love this.
"Just remember, when they say no, you hear yes and act accordingly. Someone says to you, we can't do this movie, and you hug him and say, thank you for believing in me." (34:00)

Forgive your parents.
Arnold's father was abusive, but he learned to forgive and let go through understanding.

"Would I have left Austria? Probably not, and that is my great fear. I became Arnold because of what he did to me. I recognized that I could channel my upbringing in a positive way rather than complain." (59:00)
