Founders #152: Katharine Graham - Washington Post

She risked everything to publish the Pentagon Papers and the Watergate story while facing threats from the Nixon admin.

Thrust into leadership after discovering her husband's suicide, she became the first female Fortune 500 CEO.

Here are Katharine Graham’s best ideas:

Let a major crisis divide your life into two.
Her husband’s suicide became the clear turning point that forced her into leadership after years in the background.

Many defining careers have a distinct before-and-after moment. (14:00)

The right teacher compresses years of learning into months.
With almost no business experience, Graham received patient instruction from Warren Buffett, who taught her finance step by step and helped build her confidence. (throughout)

Orangutan Theory.
Warren Buffett served as her sounding board, using Charlie Munger’s “orangutan theory.”

Simply listening while she talked helped her organize her thoughts and remove mental distortions. (57:00)
