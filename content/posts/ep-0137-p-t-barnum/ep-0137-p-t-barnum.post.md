Founders #137: P.T. Barnum

“The noblest art is that of making others happy.”

Barnum was the greatest showman of the 19th-century.

He built his fortune by spotting contradictions in human behavior and turning them into dollars.

Here are Barnum’s best ideas:

Watch actions, not words.
Barnum is running a highly profitable lottery business in a strict, religious New England town. 

He notices that the same devout citizens and church leaders who publicly condemn gambling are secretly his best customers.

Always consider the speaker's incentives. (10:00)

P.T. on effort.
"A person must make 30 hours out of 24 or he will never get ahead." - P.T. Barnum (36:00)

Stay in your circle of competence.
Barnum went bankrupt by investing outside his entertainment expertise.

He later worked his way back up, and became a charitable man. (48:00)
