Founders: #102 Akio Morita - $SONY

“If you go through life convinced that your way is always best, all the new ideas in the world will pass you by.”

From the rubble of a bombed-out warehouse to a global titan, Sony is the definitive story of Japan’s return to the world stage: 

When Sony started, the global perception of Japanese-made goods was terrible. Sony’s series of global hit products, such as the Walkman, helped change “Made in Japan” from a stigma to the highest mark of quality. (36:00)

Founder vs Founder debate on market research:

Morita did not believe in market research. “Our plan is to lead the public with new products rather than ask them what kind of products they want... The public does not know what is possible, but we do.” (39:15)

This idea contrasts with David Ogilvy, known as the “godfather of advertising,” who advised relying on your market research, much like a surgeon does:

“Why should our clients be expected to bet millions of dollars on your intuition? This willful refusal to learn the rudiments of the craft is all too common.” - Founders #82 (15:20, 38:05)

Ogilvy’s belief in market research has a key nuance: “Use research as a drunk uses a lamppost, not for illumination but for support.”

I believe Morita’s take on market research is more pointed toward the market research dogma that likely fails to grasp Ogilvy’s consideration.

Does relying on data kill the ability to build things people didn't know they wanted?

Physical neglect is visible in the mirror (a feedback loop), mental neglect is invisible but just as damaging. (48:45)

1) Hang out with enthusiastic young people
2) Vigorous exercise
3) Maintain confidence in yourself (52:00)
