Founders #74: Andrew Carnegie (Carnegie Steel)

Hire people who know more than you and study human nature (17:33).

Carnegie had “manic optimism.” A common theme among these subjects, for entrepreneurs, the benefits far outweigh the drawbacks of thinking this way. (23:45)

Violent execution of priorities, don’t wait for excuses. (25:07)

There are always opportunities to apply new knowledge for good. Don’t let what you learn gather dust. This is easier than it sounds, and considering whether applying your intellectual interests is worth your limited free time can be very helpful. (29:51)

Carnegie encourages young people to focus on attracting the highest achievers and do whatever it takes to work with them. For him, this approach was transformative, especially when he met his most important mentor, Thomas Scott. (33:08)

“If I check them every day, that’s attention away from my business.” It’s a significant time sink, especially trying to do it while working a full-time job. Unless you truly love trading (in that case, you should be doing it for work). The real issue is the stress and time involved, not underperformance versus ETFs/indices. (45:10)

“The most costly thing to pay with is time.” But don’t order delivery unless you’re raking it in because 70% of these legends obsess over costs above all else, including Carnegie. (46:22)

Carnegie on anxiety: “It is folly to cross a bridge until you come to it.” (47:07)
