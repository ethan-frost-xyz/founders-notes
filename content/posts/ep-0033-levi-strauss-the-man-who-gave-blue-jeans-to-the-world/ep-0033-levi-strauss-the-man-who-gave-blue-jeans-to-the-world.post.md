Founders #33: Levi Strauss (Levi Jeans)

When Levi Strauss ventured West, he faced a journey of 3 to 6 months with no guarantee of success. These dangerous, one-way journeys are no longer a reality for most of us.

The risk is contained nowadays, yet human nature still creates the same apprehension. The only actual difference is that today, you can decide 12 days into a "journey" to turn around.

When you set out to try something new, doubt is inevitable. I believe a key to success in modern society is to stick things out. Would you ask the captain to turn around? Hell no.
