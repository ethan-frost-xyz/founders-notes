Founders #121: Alfred Sloan and Billy Durant (General Motors)

Billy Durant built $GM with speed and vision.

Alfred Sloan turned the chaos into the most successful corporation of its time.

Here’s what I pulled from the episode:

Control the vertical that matters most.

Durant got burned early when a supplier tried to cut him out after he had already sold the product.

That lesson never left him.

Control the critical links in the supply chain or stay vulnerable forever. (21:00)

Raise your standards when you see higher ones.

Henry Leland tore into Sloan’s bearings for poor quality.

Sloan did not defend himself.

He raised his own standards on the spot.

For Leland quality was God. Sloan made it his too. (38:00)

Respect others' time.

Billy Durant treated every minute of his own time as sacred.

Wasting one was an affront to the Divine Creator.

Yet he regularly wasted the time of his best executives.

That hypocrisy drove talent away. (41:00)

Companies die from indigestion more than starvation.

Durant and $GM swallowed more than twenty companies in two years with almost no integration or controls.

The company fought off backruptcy soon thereafter.

David Packard & $HPE were right: more companies die from indigestion than starvation. (49:30)

Go to the front lines yourself.

Sloan traveled the country, sat across dealers’ desks, listened to every complaint and suggestion, took careful notes, and studied them when he got home.

Truth lives at the point of sale. (1:17:00)

Change never stops.

No company ever stopped changing.

Your job is to build a framework that turns constant change into progress instead of destruction. (1:18:00)
