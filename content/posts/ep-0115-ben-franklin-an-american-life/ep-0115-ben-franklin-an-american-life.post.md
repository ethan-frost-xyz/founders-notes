Founders #115 Ben Franklin

Young Franklin cured his argumentative nature by adopting the Socratic method.

He found that humble inquiry guides others to your point far more effectively than aggressive debate.

The lesson: Stop the abrupt contradiction. Instead simply ask why.

Franklin's ideas of success and growth.
Success is measured by the positive utility provided to the public. (9:00)

Growth is the direct result of gaining knowledge, and conquering adversity. (16:30)

The more you learn, the more interesting you become.

-> The nuance lies in discovering what interests you; don’t push a rope trying to learn fine art. There is a balance between authenticity and external appeal. (23:15)

Mediocrity is always invisible until passion shows up and exposes it. (26:00)

Reputation is built on both reality and perception.

Franklin was strategic about when he performed visible acts of hard work as a signal of reliability.

He understood the tradeoff between when to “show up” and when his time was better spent on other tasks. (28:30)

Franklin's favorite truths.

When the well is dry, we know the worth of the water.

There was never a good knife made of bad steel.

Diligence is the mother of good luck. (37:30)

Lost time is never found again. (44:30)
