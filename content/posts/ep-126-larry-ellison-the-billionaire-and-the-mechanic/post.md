Founders #126: Larry Ellison - $ORCL

“Your garden is not complete until there's nothing else you can take out of it.” - Ellison

Larry applied it to life: remove everything unnecessary so only the people and work that truly matter remain.

Here’s the three best ideas from the episode:

Use Competition to Test Yourself
Larry treated competition as a deliberate tool to measure and expand his limits, whether in business battles or extreme sports like the America’s Cup (top sailing tournament). (16:00)

Seek Out Alternative Stress
Even as one of the richest men alive he refused a relaxed life. His dangerous hobbies were a “constant search for alternative stress” to keep the same edge he had when fighting for survival. (20:00)

Born With the Right Disadvantages
“I was born with all the disadvantages needed to succeed.” His difficult early life gave him exactly the hunger and resilience required to build an empire. (1:01:00)
