Founders #136: Estée Lauder

Estée Lauder built a beauty empire through bold risks, innovative simplicity, and unyielding persistence.

Lauder implanted habits into the beauty industry that still exist today.

Here are Estée’s best ideas:

"Risk-taking is the cornerstone of empires. No one ever became a success without taking chances." (16:00)

On the ideas that built her beauty empire.
"It was simple. Most good ideas sparkle in simplicity, so much that everyone wonders why no one ever did that before." (29:00)

Some of the simple ideas she pioneered:

Word-of-mouth sales.
She relied on supreme confidence in the quality of her products rather than flashy marketing campaigns.

Free samples.
By simplifying the sales process to simply letting women try the cream, the product effectively sold itself.

Consumer habits.
Women did not typically buy their own beauty products due to social norms, but Lauder changed that through marketing. This simple shift in perception turned a rare luxury gift into a daily household staple, driving massive sales volume. (33:00)

On perseverance.
"It's that quality that forces you to persevere. Find the route around the stone wall. It's the immovable stubbornness that will not allow you to cave in when everyone says, give up." - Lauder (31:00)

How she stopped corporate espionage.
"I never give the whole formula. When the mixture is 95% or even 98% completed and the great vats of perfume are ready to be bottled, one member of the Lauder family goes to the factory to supply the missing secret 5% or 2%, the ingredient without which the fragrance can never be complete." (35:00)
