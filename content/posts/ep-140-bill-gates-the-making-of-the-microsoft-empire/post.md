Founders #140: Bill Gates

Gates viewed every missed contract as a double loss: revenue lost to Microsoft and opportunities given to competitors. (43:00)

My take: Treat opportunities as if you're trying to make the playoffs, playing every game against your division rival.

Work with people who are hardcore.
“It was when we got that free time that we really got into computers... Then I became hardcore. It was day and night.” 

Gates was 13 at the time. (10:00)

Surround yourself with people who will push you.
While at Harvard, Gates was hesitant about fully committing to starting a software company. Despite his obvious passion for computers, he was paralyzed.

Friend and future co-founder Paul Allen showed Gates a magazine featuring the Altair 880, which marked the beginning of the personal computer revolution. (18:00)

Allen cited a quote from Shakespeare's Julius Caesar that convinced him to start Microsoft:

“There is a tide in the affairs of men, which, taken at the flood, leads on to Fortune. Omitted, all the voyage of their life is bound in shallows and in miseries.” - William Shakespeare

Gates on complacency.
“Believe me... Staring out the window and saying, ‘isn't this great,’ is not the way to push things forward. You've got to keep driving hard.” (54:00)
