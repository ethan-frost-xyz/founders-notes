Founders #50 Marc Andreessen

The market is the most critical factor in a startup's success or failure, beating out both the caliber of the team and the quality of the product. A great market with lots of customers will pull the product out of the startup, but a terrible market will kill even the best team with the best product. (14:30)

Don’t plan your career. The world changes too quickly for you to predict what will happen. It is better to treat your career like an investment portfolio where you balance risks and are always ready to seize opportunities that present themselves spontaneously. (36:00 - 50:00)

When Thomas Edison invented the phonograph, the New York Times predicted it would be used for "bottled sermons" where wealthy people would collect recorded religious speeches, almost no one initially foresaw it becoming a device for mass entertainment. (32:30)

You should view big companies like Moby Dick because their internal decision-making is complex and completely unpredictable to outsiders. Never start a company that requires a partnership with a big corporation to succeed, as they can easily capsize you without even meaning to. (20:30)
