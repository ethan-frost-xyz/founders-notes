Founders #23: Jim Clark (Netscape)

“You reach these points in life where you say, fuck, I’ve reached some sort of dead end here. What was the point of getting here? … All those years you thought you were achieving something, and you achieved nothing.” (29:22) Clark was 38 at the time. It’s never too late to become a billionaire. Clark soon thereafter founded Netscape with Marc Andreessen, who Senra recommends as an “interesting” follow on Twitter (33:26). Given he was beefing with the Pope on here just a couple days ago, I cannot agree more with the rec. 

Clark argued there were two types of people: 1) the pig, who is committed; and 2) the chicken, who is interested. “If you are going to do anything worth doing, you need a lot of pigs.” I think the point here is commitment versus curiosity and the importance of being able to decipher who is what when hiring. (43:30)

Adding to my first point, the guy basically hit rock bottom at 38 after his second divorce, then one night decided to lock in after facing the worst two years of his life (he had a terrible childhood btw). Clark was always trying to prove everyone wrong. What a beauty.
