Founders #133: Edwin Land - Polaroid

Land dedicated 30 years to perfecting his revolutionary camera.

Like his mentee, Steve Jobs, Land's life shows that monumental success requires decades of relentless persistence and a refusal to accept the status quo.

Here are more of Land’s best ideas:

Remember that you will die someday, and your fear will die today.
"Almost everything, all external expectations, all pride, all fear of embarrassment or failure, these things just fall away in the face of death, leaving only what is truly important." - Land (6:00)

Nature is indifferent to your struggles. 
The physical world yields only to ingenuity and imagination, not effort or wishful thinking

"Nature offered no bar to the elegant solution over the awkward compromise if they had the imagination to seek it and the wit to discover it." - Land (8:00)

Bet on the great individual, not the group. 
Progress leaps forward through obsessed outliers who compress lifetimes of advancement into one career.

This podcast is evidence of such phenomenon. (17:00)

Question everything like a child. 
Unquestioned norms hide massive opportunities. Test cliches and reject conventional wisdom. (31:00)

Edwin Land on taste.
"Taste is as rare as the unicorn." - Land (41:00)

Make learning a lifelong duty.
"The individual and industry will be better qualified... if he continues his education as an integral part of his working career." - Land (43:00)
